CC 9 - Analytic Charts

Section 1: FDI Inflow and Number of SEZs Across Provinces in Indonesia

This regression chart illustrates the relationship between regional minimum wage (x-axis) and FDI inflow (y-axis) across Indonesia's provinces in 2022. While the fitted regression line shows a slight negative trend, many data points (dots) are scattered far from the line, indicating significant variation in FDI inflow that cannot be fully explained by regional minimum wage alone. The chart also incorporates a zoom-in and zoom-out feature, enabling more detailed exploration of data points by scrolling.

Section 2: Comparing Provincial FDI Inflows After SEZ Establishment.

Using "facet" encoding, I created four charts with a vertical line marking the SEZ establishment year. The charts show that, except for West Nusa Tenggara, FDI inflows increased in other provinces with a 2-5 year lag.

CC 10 - Option 1. Interactive Visualisations

Section 1: FDI Inflow and Number of SEZs Across Provinces in Indonesia

The plot chart displays FDI inflow and SEZ numbers at the provincial level in Indonesia. Two toggles allow adjustments for the year (2016–2022) and the FDI range on the x-axis. Tooltips show the province name, FDI inflow, and SEZ count, while circle size represents the population. Additionally, a feature to isolate each province is included for easier observation.

Section 2: Comparing Provincial FDI Inflows After SEZ Establishment.

This line chart illustrates the Net FDI Inflow (as a percentage of GDP) for the five largest ASEAN economies. The values have been rebased to 1975 = 100 to improve clarity, as Singapore's significantly higher values made it challenging to observe trends in the other countries. Additionally, interactive start and end year sliders have been included, allowing users to explore specific periods for a more detailed analysis.
